Intro

Ever since my junior year in high school, I've encompassed myself in two passions, programming and writing. With each passing day, I've been sharpening my skills to a solid point. Whether it be developing a video game for a school project or writing short stories, I've never stopped surrounding myself with the things I love!

Throughout the years I've been learning and developing my skills with programming and software engineering. I'm proficient in Java, C++, Python with experience in HTML, CSS, Javascript, and C#. Additionally, I'm well aquainted with Linux and Bash. I've used these languages to dive into my passions headfirst, aspiring to become a game developer. In addition to that, I've also written short stories and am even in the process of writing a novel! Writing is about twice as addicting as reading can be, so long as the story is an awe inspiring journey! I'm looking to write my own story and I won't stop for anyone.

Work

Game Development is a passion of mine, and a career path, I aspire to. Not only can developers lavish in their enjoyment of promgramming, but they're also the writers of incredible stories!

Of course, like most things, there's no fun in programming and writing unless you have someone to share it with. The above photo is a headshot for a character from the first full game I ever developed. It was a group project in my junior year of highschool. While the guidelines were very loose, my friends and I decided to work on developing our first ever parkour game. We called it Darwin Inc, the name stemming from the core gameplay concept where the player would collect power-ups to mutate their body in order to complete the parkour. I worked on both drawing the assets used within the game and programming elements within the game itself. Needless to say, our class loved the game and it even won first place in our class competition!

In addition to that, I've also worked on a few short stories in the past, my personal favorite being "The Girl Who Cried Monster" which follows a monsterous observer. This observer feels the dreadful emotions of a young girl and seeks to help her, but can she get past the fact that he's a monster? And can he accept her rejection? Besides those short stories, I'm also in the process of writing a novel that follows four adventurous souls as they travel across the ocean in search of the fountain of youth. This story takes inspiration from Frankenstein by Mary Shelley and Dracula by Bram Stoker, taking heavy inspiration from their formatting and writing styles!

About

I never once walked this road of mine alone. I always found it important to surround yourself with people who care. The guys in this photo helped me develop my first game in highschool and I still talk to them regularly today. It's these type of friends you carry with you for your whole life.
